# islet-size-heterogeneity-T2D
Using multiplex imaging mass cytometry (IMC), we systematically analyzed human pancreatic islets across non-diabetic (ND), prediabetic (PreD), and type 2 diabetic (T2D) donors. Binary masks for insulin (INS), glucagon (GCG), somatostatin (SST), and pancreatic polypeptide (PP) were summed to generate a composite endocrine mask. Morphological holes were filled based on four markers binary masks (see scripts/islet_mask_generate.r). Endocrine cell identity was assigned using the mask-area ratios of INS, GCG, SST, and PPY, defining β, α, δ, and PP cells, respectively (see scripts/cell_annotation.m). For each segmented cell, the mean staining intensity of every antibody channel was quantified, and the cell's x–y centroid coordinates were recorded, yielding a single-cell-by-channel expression matrix with paired spatial coordinates(see scripts/cell_position_intensity.r). To characterize phenotypic heterogeneity among medium and large islets across diabetes stages, we clustered these islets from ND, PreD, and T2D donors by t-SNE (see scripts/tSNE_cluster.m). The resulting clusters were then ordered with Monocle3 to infer the probable sequence of phenotypic transitions across disease stages (see scripts/monocle3.r).
